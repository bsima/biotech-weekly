Kate's Favorites

From relativity to quantum theory – our physical world explored through coffee // Aeon Videos

A collaboration between the French filmmaker Charlotte Arene and the University of Paris-Sud’s Laboratory of Solid State Physics, Physics and Caffeine explores how the best models for understanding the physical world – from electromagnetism to special relativity to quantum mechanics – converge in every cup of coffee.

Targeting [beta]1-integrin signaling enhances regeneration in aged and dystrophic muscle in mice // Nature Medicine

Non-OA | Why do aged muscles heal slowly? [beta]1-integrin in the niche contributes to muscle stem cell maintenance via Fgf2 signaling, and during aging, stimulation of this integrin promotes muscle stem cell responsiveness to this growth factor and improved muscle regeneration.

23andMe Sells Data for Drug Search // MIT Tech Review

The consumer genetic-testing startup has amassed one of the world’s largest databases of DNA. Now it is sifting through it for clues to new drugs.

Early-life nutrition modulates the epigenetic state of specific rDNA genetic variants in mice // Science

Non-OA | The process by which a mother's diet during pregnancy can permanently affect her offspring's attributes, such as weight, could be strongly influenced by genetic variation in an unexpected part of the genome, in mice.

Adjuvant-dependent innate and adaptive immune signatures of risk of SIVmac251 acquisition // Nature Medicine

Non-OA | Vaccari et al. report that SIV vaccines formulated with two different adjuvants elicit distinct immune responses and effects on SIV acquisition in rhesus macaques.
