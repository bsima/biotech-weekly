Research and Development

NANOG Reverses the Myogenic Differentiation Potential of Senescent Stem Cells by Restoring ACTIN Filamentous Organization and SRF-Dependent Gene Expression // Stem Cells

In a series of experiments, the embryonic stem cell gene Nanog kicked into action dormant cellular processes that are key to preventing weak bones, clogged arteries and other telltale signs of growing old.

Feedback-Controlled Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation Reveals a Functional Role of Sleep Spindles in Motor Memory Consolidation // Current Biology

Non-OA | For the first time, scientists report using transcranial alternating current stimulation, or tACS, to target a specific kind of brain activity during sleep and strengthen memory in healthy people.

Intestinal cell damage and systemic immune activation in individuals reporting sensitivity to wheat in the absence of coeliac disease // Gut

OA | These findings reveal a state of systemic immune activation in conjunction with a compromised intestinal epithelium affecting a subset of individuals who experience sensitivity to wheat in the absence of coeliac disease.

Key findings on how Americans view new technologies that could ‘enhance’ human abilities // Pew Research Center

OA | Americans are more worried than enthusiastic about using gene editing, brain chip implants and synthetic blood to change human capabilities

Ancient Bone Shows Evidence Of Cancer In Human Ancestor // NPR

At about 1.6 million years old, the bone is the oldest known example of a malignant tumor in a human ancestor, and it reinforces what scientists already knew: that cancer isn't a modern scourge. OA | Earliest hominin cancer: 1.7-million-year-old osteosarcoma from Swartkrans Cave, South Africa
