Chromosomal Instability and Molecular Defects in Induced Pluripotent Stem Cells from Nijmegen Breakage Syndrome Patients // Cell Reports

OA | Halevy et al. describe molecular defects in induced pluripotent stem cells derived from Nijmegen breakage syndrome (NBS) patients and find mitotic and apoptotic blocks leading to chromosomal instability. NBS-neural progenitor cells reveal downregulation of neural genes correlated with low P53 levels, which may underlie severe microcephaly observed in patients.

Combination therapy shows promise for chronic myeloid leukemia // MD Anderson Cancer Center

Brain connections are more sophisticated than thought // Duke University

An in vivo chemico-genetic proximity-labeling approach was used to discover inhibitory postsynaptic proteins. Quantitative mass spectrometry confirmed known inhibitory postsynaptic proteins and revealed a large network of new proteins, many of which are either implicated in neurodevelopmental disorders or are of unknown function.

A Tenth of the World’s Wilderness Lost since the 1990s // Wildlife Conservation Society

An estimated 3.3 million km2 (almost 10 percent) of wilderness area has been lost in the intervening years. Those losses have occurred primarily in South America, which has experienced a 30 percent decline in wilderness, and Africa, which has experienced a 14 percent loss.
