Thousands of DNA droplets for diagnostics // ScienceDaily

A team of scientists has developed a method for dividing a DNA sample into thousands of tiny droplets. The process is easier to control with rapid generation of more than 10,000 droplets with a diameter of approximately 120 micrometers each. Applicable anywhere DNA is used to diagnose.

New breath test for pneumonia // Manchester University

Proof-of-concept study showing that chemical analysis of breath samples can reveal bacterial infections in the lower respiratory tract. Potential for huge effect on clinical practice as healthcare associated infections are a major issue worldwide. Received funding for three for years of work from the National Institute for Health Research Invention.

Cancer Drug Scorecard, ASCO Releases Value Framework

A framework to assess and compare cancer treatment options. It has been dubbed the "Cancer Drug Scorecard" and is designed to be used to find a rational relationship between the health benefit of an intervention and its cost. Scores of a treatment range from 0-100, with bonus points.

Big Pharma might not be too happy when they get their report card.

Devices

Implantable ‘artificial pancreas’ could help diabetes patients control their blood sugar // American Chemical Society

Researchers at the University of California Santa Barbara designed an algorithm that monitors blood sugar levels and computes an insulin dose that it delivers quickly and automatically when necessary. The algorithm is designed to work with implanted devices, specifically with an artificial pancreas, and would overcome the delays experienced with current devices. Researchers say they will soon test the device in animals.

One step closer to a hands-off diabetes treatment.

Design and Evaluation of a Robust PID Controller for a Fully Implantable Artificial Pancreas

New conductive ink for electronic apparel // EurekAlert! Science News

University of Tokyo researchers have developed a new ink that can be printed on textiles in a single step to form highly conductive and stretchable connections. Functional ink will enable electronic apparel like sportswear and underwear with sensing devices for measuring a range of biological indicators such as heart rate or muscle contraction.

Tissue Engineering

Discovery of nanotubes offers new clues about cell-to-cell communication // UT Southwestern

“This finding stands to change the way we think about how stem cells and their neighbors communicate with one another,” said Dr. Michael Buszczak with the Hamon Center for Regenerative Science and Medicine. He and his research group have found that certain types of stem cells use microscopic, threadlike nanotubes to communicate with neighboring cells.

Kind of like your friend that still uses landlines and refuses to join the world of wireless smartphones.

Researchers mass-producing stem cells to satisfy the demands of regenerative medicine // PHYS

Preferring microcarriers to petri dishes, a research group out of A*STAR Bioprocessing Technology Institute are working on developing a scalable method for growing stem cells and differentiated cells. The team is partnering with industry to further improve the process, as well as with clinicians to test the healing potential of their cells on animal models
