Kate's Favorites

U.S. Military Preps for Gene Drives Run Amok // Scientific American

DARPA researchers are developing responses for accidental or malicious “genetic spills”

DNA Methylation Dynamics of Human Hematopoietic Stem Cell Differentiation // Stem Cell

OA | Scientists have established comprehensive maps of the human epigenome, shedding light on how the body regulates which genes are active in which cells. Over the last five years, a worldwide consortium of scientists has established epigenetic maps of 2,100 cell types.

Developmentally programmed germ cell remodelling by endodermal cell cannibalism // Nature Cell Biology

OA | In living beings, from roundworms to humans, some cells may ball up unwanted contents on their surfaces for other cells to "eat." 

Research and Development

In vivo genome editing via CRISPR/Cas9 mediated homology-independent targeted integration // Nature Research

Non-OA | Researchers have developed the ability to insert DNA at a target location into the non-dividing cells that make up the majority of adult organs and tissues. The technique was able to partially restore visual responses in blind rodents.

The C. elegans Taste Receptor Homolog LITE-1 Is a Photoreceptor // Cell

Non-OA | A taste receptor homolog absorbs UV light and mediates avoidance behavior in C. elegans 
in response to light exposure.

Lifestyle chemistries from phones for individual profiling // PNAS

OA |  The concept of skin-associated lifestyle chemistries found on personal belongings as a form of trace evidence to solve crimes by building a profile. 

SYP73 Anchors the ER to the Actin Cytoskeleton for Maintenance of ER Integrity and Streaming in Arabidopsis // Current Biology

Non-OA | Cao et al. identify a new player involved in the connection between the ER and actin
in plant cells. SYP73 is an ER membrane-associated actin-binding protein. Overexpression
of SYP73 reshapes ER to overlay actin, while SYP73 loss-of-function mutant displays
defects in both plant development and ER dynamics.

Error-Robust Modes of the Retinal Population Code // PLOS Computational Biology

OA | researchers used visual stimuli to activate groups of about 150 retinal ganglion cells with overlapping visual regions and recorded their response. They used the data to build a mathematical model of retinal ganglion activity and analyze its patterns and organization.

Single-cell RNA-seq ties macrophage polarization to growth rate of intracellular Salmonella // Nature Microbiology

Non-OA | Single-cell RNA-seq finds a pro-inflammatory M1 polarized immune response in macrophages infected with actively replicating Salmonella and an anti-inflammatory M2-like response in uninfected bystanders or cells with non-replicating bacteria.

Systematic review and statistical analysis of the integrity of 33 randomized controlled trials // Neurology

OA | A systematic approach using statistical techniques to assess randomization outcomes can evaluate data integrity, in this case suggesting these RCT results may be unreliable.

Long Looks

Good Health Habits Can Reduce Genetic Risk For Heart Attack // NPR

People with unlucky genes but good health habits were half as likely to develop heart disease as those who had an unhealthful lifestyle and genes that increased their heart risk, a study found.

This Simple Philosophical Puzzle Shows How Difficult It Is to Know Something // Nautilus

In the 1960s, the American philosopher Edmund Gettier devised a thought experiment that has become known as a “Gettier case."

Stephen Hawking just gave humanity a due date for finding another planet // The Washington Post

The theoretical physicist says the threats to humanity are manifold — and "escaping beyond our fragile planet" is urgent.
