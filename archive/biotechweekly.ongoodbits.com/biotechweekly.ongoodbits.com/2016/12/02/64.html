Research and Development

Impaired Amino Acid Transport at the Blood Brain Barrier Is a Cause of Autism Spectrum Disorder // Cell

Sci-Hub | Mutations in a solute carrier protein that blunts the transport of branched-chain amino acids lead to neurological and behavioral abnormalities associated with autism spectrum disorder.

Phase I trial shows that a drug that inhibits the Notch signalling process is active in a range of advanced cancers // ENA2016 NEWS

After treatment with LY3039478, the tumour shrank in one patient with breast cancer and the disease stabilised and did not progress in another 29 patients. In addition, using PET scanning, researchers found two more cases where the tumours had shrunk: in a patient with adenoid cystic carcinoma and a patient with testicular cancer.

Nanochannel Implants for Minimally-Invasive Insertion and Intratumoral Delivery // Biomedical Nanatechnology

Sci-Hub | A capsule that delivers medicinal doses through over 5000 nanochannels for several days or a few weeks. Could be used for any kind of ailment that needs a localized delivery over several days or a few weeks. 

Allen Cell Collection

The first publicly available collection of gene edited, fluorescently tagged human induced pluripotent stem cells that target key cellular structures with unprecedented clarity.

These Wearables Detect Health Issues Before They Happen // MIT Tech Review

Technologies created by the federally funded MD2K project could lead to consumer devices that offer health guidance in real time.

Clonal fate mapping quantifies the number of haematopoietic stem cells that arise during development // Nature Cell Biology

Shared Access | Approximately 21 HSC clones exist prior to HSC emergence and 30 clones are present during peak production from aortic endothelium. Stress haematopoiesis, including sublethal irradiation and transplantation, reduces clonal diversity
