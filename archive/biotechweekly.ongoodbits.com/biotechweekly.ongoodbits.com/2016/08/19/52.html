Kate's Favorites

Structure-based discovery of opioid analgesics with reduced side effects // Nature

Non-OA | The newly deciphered atomic structure of the brain's "morphine receptor" was used to custom-engineer a novel drug candidate that blocked pain as effectively as morphine in mouse experiments, but did not interfere with breathing, and appears to side-step the brain's dopamine-driven addiction circuitry.

Family Guy Cartoon Blows it on Stem Cells for Stroke // The Niche

The animated show Family Guy weighed in on stem cells when main character Peter Griffin has a stroke that is then shown to be miraculously healed by a stem cell research facility of some kind that sure looks like a stem cell clinic.

What Does Half-Life Mean? // Scientific American

What are half-lives? And what do they have to do with measuring the age of the solar system and predicting the effects of a morning cup of coffee?

Research and Development

Live Cell Imaging Reveals the Dynamics of Telomerase Recruitment to Telomeres // Cell

Non-OA | Live cell imaging studies reveal how telomerase scouts for telomeres in the 3D nuclear space through multiple dynamic touch-and-go interactions.

Exogenous TNFR2 activation protects from acute GvHD via host T reg cell expansion // JAM

Non-OA | The STAR2 protein was developed to stimulate the formation of the transplant recipient's own T reg cells in vivo. Pretreating mice with STAR2 protected them from developing GvHD after immune cell transplantation. The donor-derived cells retained their ability to kill the recipient's lymphoma cells.

Comparison of Patient-Reported Outcomes in 5-Year Survivors Who Received Bone Marrow vs Peripheral Blood Unrelated Donor Transplantation:  Long-term Follow-up of a Randomized Clinical Trial // JAMA

Non-OA | This follow-up of a randomized clinical trial compares patient-reported outcomes between patients randomized to receive unrelated donor bone marrow or peripheral blood hematopoietic cell transplantation for hematologic cancers.

This Wristband Can Trace Your GPS Directions on Your Skin // MIT Tech Revoew

“You actually feel a shape being drawn on your skin as if someone was taking their finger and tracing out a triangle or an arrow in a certain direction,” cofounder and CEO Shantanu Bala says

Spectroscopic and computational investigation of actinium coordination chemistry // Nature

OA | "To build an appropriate biological delivery system for actinium, there is a clear need to better establish the chemical fundamentals for actinium," Ferrier said

Using the Internet to access information inflates future use of the Internet to access other information // Memory
