World Biotech News

Greek scientists lose access to digital journals // Nature News

The country's economic crisis is closing in hard on researchers.

Maybe after greek access to journals is reinstated, we could work on getting more of those journals open access...

Genetic testing company 23andMe raises $80 million // Fortune

DNA testing and genome sequencing startup 23andMe has raised a fresh $80 million in new funding. If you recall, in 2013 the company was forced to stop selling their kits but early this year the FDA approved use of the kits for the genetic testing of Bloom Syndrome. The company also plans to develop its own drugs using the massive amounts of data it’s collecting from customers.

A whole new meaning to personalized medicine.

US vaccine researcher sentenced to prison for fraud // Nature News

On July 1, Dong-Pyou Han, a former biomedical scientist at Iowa State University in Ames, was sentenced to 57 months for fabricating and falsifying data in HIV vaccine trials. Han has also been fined US$7.2 million and will be subject to three years of supervised release after he leaves prison.

NHS to give volunteers 'synthetic blood' made in laboratory within two years

The first attempt at giving human volunteers “synthetic blood” made in a laboratory for the first time will take place within the next two years, the NHS has announced.

Safer, with more benefits: parents’ vaccine views shifting // University of Michigan Health System

A quarter of parents believe vaccines are safer now than they thought a year ago; a third more supportive of school and daycare immunization requirements.

Real science seems to be making a comeback.
