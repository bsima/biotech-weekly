Research and Development

New research shows how to trap cancer by turning your body against the tumour // The Conversation

Novel drugs that reduce the spread of cancer in mice could pave the way for changing the way we fight tumours.

Hypoxia and loss of PHD2 inactivate stromal fibroblasts to decrease tumour stiffness and metastasis
OA

Scientists produce clearest-ever images of enzyme that plays key roles in aging, cancer // UCLA

“Many details we could only guess at before, we can now see unambiguously, and we now have an understanding of where the different components of telomerase interact,” said Juli Feigon, a professor of chemistry and biochemistry in the UCLA College and a senior author of the study.

Structure of Tetrahymena telomerase reveals previously unknown subunits, functions, and interactions
Non-OA

Special Class of T Cells Shown to Both Attack Cancer Cells and Enlist Other Immune Cells // Roswell Park Cancer Institute

Asubset of CD4+ “helper” T cells, called “tumor-recognizing CD4+ T cells” or TR-CD4 because of their unique ability to recognize and respond to cancer cells, has been identified. These special CD4+ T cells are antigen-specific to NY-ESO-1, an antigen expressed by many solid tumors including ovarian, melanoma, prostate, lung, breast and synovial sarcoma cancers.

Direct tumor recognition by a human CD4 T-cell subset potently mediates tumor growth inhibition and orchestrates anti-tumor immune responses
OA

Researchers find neural switch that turns dreams on and off // Berkeley News

The researchers inserted an optogenetic switch into a group of nerve cells located in the ancient part of the brain called the medulla, allowing them to activate or inactivate the neurons with laser light.

Control of REM sleep by ventral medulla GABAergic neurons
Non-OA

Scientists Convert Skin Cells Into Functional Placenta-Generating Cells // The Hebrew University of Jerusalem

Three genes (Gata3, Eomes and Tfap2c) were introduced into skin cells to initiate a cell fate program that completely converted the skin cells into stable and fully functional placenta-generating cells. These skin-derived TSCs look like native TSCs, and function and contribute to developing placenta.

Extensive Nuclear Reprogramming Underlies Lineage Conversion into Functional Trophoblast Stem-like Cells

Team describes rapid, sensitive test for HIV mutations // Brown

Researchers describes a new method that works faster and more sensitively in lab testing than the current standard technologies to distinguish whether HIV-positive people are infected with a drug-resistant strain or a non-resistant strain. This allows patients to get the most effective treatment as quickly as possible.

One-Step Ligation on RNA Amplification for the Detection of Point Mutations

Engineers create artificial skin that can send pressure sensation to brain cell // Stanford

Stanford engineers have created a plastic skin-like material that can detect pressure and deliver a Morse code-like signal directly to a living brain cell.

Restoring the sense of touch

3D scans spot earliest signs of heart disease // MRC Clinical Sciences Centre

Specialised MRI scans create a 3D map of the heart that was used to explore and establish the precise changes in shape and function – the effects of increasing blood pressure.

Precursors of Hypertensive Heart Phenotype Develop in Healthy Adults : A High-Resolution 3D MRI Study

Dietary Supplements Send Thousands To ERs Yearly // Health News : NPR

More than 23,000 Americans end up in emergency rooms each year after taking dietary supplements, an analysis shows. Most cases are linked to weight-loss products or energy-boosting supplements.
