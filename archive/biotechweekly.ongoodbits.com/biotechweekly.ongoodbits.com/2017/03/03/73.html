DNA Fountain enables a robust and efficient storage architecture // Science

Sci-hub | A method, DNA Fountain, approaches the theoretical maximum for information stored per nucleotide.

Erlich and his colleague Zielinski chose six files to encode, or write, into DNA: a full computer operating system, an 1895 French film, "Arrival of a train at La Ciotat," a $50 Amazon gift card, a computer virus, a Pioneer plaque and a 1948 study by information theorist Claude Shannon.

The files were compressed and split into short strings of binary code made up of ones and zeros. An erasure-correcting algorithm called fountain codes was used to randomly package the strings into "droplets". The ones and zeros in each droplet were mapped to the four nucleotide bases in DNA: A, G, C and T.

A digital list of 72,000 DNA strands, each 200 bases long was sent it in a text file to become DNA molecules. To retrieve their files, they sequenced  the DNA strands and translated the genetic code back into binary. Erlich opens his archived operating system on a virtual machine and plays a game of Minesweeper to celebrate.

Research and Development

Assembly of embryonic and extra-embryonic stem cells to mimic embryogenesis in vitro // Science

Sci-hub | Genetically-modified stem cells and specific inhibitors allow  embryogenesis of mouse embryonic stem cell and trophoblast stem cell derived embryos: ETS-embryos, depending on crosstalk involving Nodal signaling. When ETS-embryos develop, they spontaneously initiate expression of mesoderm and primordial germ cell markers asymmetrically on the embryonic and extra-embryonic border, in response to Wnt and BMP signaling. 

Nanoengineers 3-D print biomimetic blood vessel networks // Biomaterials

Sci-hub | Chen's lab has 3D printed a vasculature network that can safely integrate with the body's own network to circulate blood. These blood vessels branch out into many series of smaller vessels, similar to the blood vessel structures found in the body.

Shared Gene Expression Alterations in Nasal and Bronchial Epithelium for Lung Cancer Detection // JNCI

Non-OA | Less invasive (than the lung) test looks at gene expression in cells in the nose to diagnose lung cancer. Built off a previous test using bronchial cells, researchers found 535 genes that were expressed differently in people with or without cancer in a study of 500 people.

Metastatic State of Cancer Cells May Be Indicated by Adhesion Strength // Cell

OA | Researchers identified Mg2+- and Ca2+-mediated differences in adhesion strength between metastatic and nonmetastatic mammary epithelial cell lines, which occur over concentration ranges similar to those found in tumor stroma.
