Kate's Favorites

A toolkit of thread-based microfluidics, sensors, and electronics for 3D tissue embedding for medical diagnostics : Microsystems & Nanoengineering

Report on thread-based microfluidic networks that interface intimately with biological tissues in three dimensions

Maths notation is needlessly complex. It can and should be better // Aeon Videos

How expressing exponents, logarithms and roots could be made simpler by using one elegant notation system, and makes a broader case for how maths could be made more accessible by developing cleaner – and perhaps even artful – notation.

Research and Development

An artificial niche preserves the quiescence of muscle stem cells and enhances their therapeutic efficacy // Nature Biotechnology

Non-OA | A molecular signature of quiescence was found using machine learning and used to screen for factors that could maintain mouse MuSC quiescence, thus defining a quiescence medium.

Spatial Embedding and Wiring Cost Constrain the Functional Layout of the Cortical Network of Rodents and Primates // PLOS Biology

OA | Larger primate brains have much weaker long-distance connections than smaller rodent brains, which could explain why large brains are more susceptible to certain mental illnesses such as schizophrenia and Alzheimer disease.

Machine Vision’s Achilles’ Heel Revealed by Google Brain Researchers // MIT Tech Review

By some measures machine vision is better than human vision. But now researchers have found a class of “adversarial images” that easily fool it.

Host-Pathogen Coevolution and the Emergence of Broadly Neutralizing Antibodies in Chronic Infections // PLOS Genetics

OA | A mathematical description of the coevolutionary process between HIV and our adaptive immune system that discovers key parameters governing the distribution of interactions between the two populations, introduces principled measures of adaptation, and analyzes the conditions under which highly potent broadly neutralizing antibodies will emerge and dominate the immune response.

Targeting [beta]1-integrin signaling enhances regeneration in aged and dystrophic muscle in mice // Nature Medicine

Non-OA | [beta]1-integrin in the niche contributes to muscle stem cell maintenance via Fgf2 signaling, and during aging, stimulation of this integrin promotes muscle stem cell responsiveness to this growth factor and improved muscle regeneration.

Modeling Zika Virus Infection in Pregnancy // NEJM

OA | Three of the four studies suggest that ZIKA gains access to the fetus during pregnancy after crossing the placental barrier, which is composed of different types of trophoblasts and ancillary cells

FDA Extends Authority to e-Cigarettes: Implications for Smoking Cessation? // JAMA

OA | How the FDA’s new authority over e-cigarettes could affect adult smoking cessation.

Martian Colonists Could Be Genetically Engineered for Democracy // Nautilus

The underlying technology for it, CRISPR Cas9—a gene-editing tool adapted from the prokaryote immune system—can precisely, easily, and cheaply snip strands of DNA, allowing for customizable genomes. With such an accessible technology, it seems likely humans will start to not just take away bad things—like illnesses transmitted by mosquitos—but also add good things.
